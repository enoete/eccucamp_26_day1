The Format Question
NOT THE SAME
CREATIVE CHALLENGE
Long-Form Brand Film
~3 MINUTES

StoryBeginning. Middle. End.

GoalMakes someone feel something over time

ShowsThe whole world of the brand

PlatformsYouTube · Website · Event screens

Short-Form Reel
15–30 SEC

StoryOne moment. One feeling.

GoalMakes someone stop scrolling

ShowsThe ONE thing that defines the brand

PlatformsInstagram · TikTok · WhatsApp

If you had 15 seconds — what single moment would you show?
⭐
Gold Star
A long-form film and a short-form reel are not the same story told at different speeds. They are fundamentally different creative challenges. Remember this every time you sit down to plan a video.
Your Turn

Read Your Brief

⏱ 15 MINUTES · CLOSE YOUR OTHER TABS
  • What is the stated ask?
  • What is the real ask — beneath what they wrote?
  • Who exactly is watching? Be specific — not just "businesses"
  • Three words: how should the long-form film make someone feel?
  • Three words: how should the reel make someone feel? Same or different?
🔴
Red Alert
Close everything else. This is thinking time, not generating time. No OpenArt, no browsers, no distractions — just you, your brief, and your notes.
⭐
Gold Star
The real ask is always beneath the stated ask. Every client in the world says "make us a video." Your job is to find out what they actually need.

WHAT IS A
MOODBOARD?
60 minutes
The Moodboard
A visual argument
for a creative direction.
It answers one question:
"What world does this film live in?"
What Goes On a Moodboard
WHAT EARNS
ITS PLACE
🎨

Colour Palette

3–5 colours that define the brand world

✍️

Typography Energy

Not a specific font — the feeling of the type. Bold? Playful? Minimal?

🖼️

Imagery

Locations, textures, people — how it FEELS, not what they DO

💡

Lighting Reference

Golden hour? Neon? Clinical white? How does this world want to be lit?

💬

Three Feeling Words

Your anchors from Session 2 — placed on the board

⭐

The Reel Moment

ONE image that could stop a scroll on its own

Important
WHAT A MOODBOARD
IS NOT
✗   A random collection of pretty pictures
✗   A Pinterest board with 40 things on it
✗   A finished design
✗   Something you make after deciding what the film looks like
⭐
Gold Star
A moodboard is a visual argument for a creative direction — not a collection of pretty pictures. If you can replace any image without it mattering, that image doesn't belong.

DIRECTION
30 minutes
Your Creative North Star
Every decision in Weeks 2 and 3
gets checked against this sentence.
If a clip doesn't serve the sentence — cut it.
If a music choice doesn't serve it — change it.
If a narration line doesn't serve it — rewrite it.
Actually — Two
ONE FOR EACH
FORMAT
Long-Form Brand Film
A film that makes _____________ feel _____________ so that they _____________________.
Social Reel
One moment — _____________________ — that makes someone ________________________.
They serve the same brand. They are not the same sentence.

Your Turn

Write Two Sentences

⏱ 10 MINUTES · PAPER OR NOTES

  • It must name a specific feeling

  • If it could apply to ANY client — rewrite it

Build Your Moodboard

  • Colour palette — 3 to 5 colours that define the brand world

While You Build

QUESTIONS

Does this feel Caribbean? Does it feel like the RIGHT island?

Quality Check

⏱ 5 MINUTES · SELF-REVIEW

  • Is the reel moment obvious — and different from the long-form feeling?
  • Are your creative direction sentences still true — or has the board taken you somewhere different?
  • What would you change if you had 30 more minutes?
